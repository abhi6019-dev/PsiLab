PsiLab evaluates the bound-state hydrogen solution ψ(n,l,m)=R(n,l)Y(l,m) in atomic units. The radial field uses the normalized Schrodinger eigenfunction with associated Laguerre polynomials, while the angular field uses normalized real spherical harmonics built from associated Legendre polynomials.

Samples are drawn from the true separable probability density. The radial distribution r²|R|² is integrated into a normalized inverse CDF, and the angular term |Y|² is accepted by rejection sampling over the unit sphere. The resulting cloud is uploaded as typed-array GPU buffers and rendered as an additive WebGL point field.

OrbitControls provide damped rotation, wheel zoom, and touch pinch. The renderer uses logarithmic depth, adaptive draw ranges, worker-thread generation, and compact shader sprites so dense states remain responsive on laptops and scale upward on stronger GPUs.

The central proton is scaled from the physical proton charge radius, about 1.589×10-5 Bohr radii. At full-cloud scale it is therefore nearly invisible by design; deep zoom reveals the physically sized nucleus while the point cloud remains a probability distribution, not enlarged electron beads.

Orientation presets expose the real harmonic basis directly: p orbitals map to pz, px, and py; d orbitals map to dz², dxz, dyz, dx²-y², and dxy. Higher angular momenta expose their cos(mφ) and sin(mφ) real orientations.
